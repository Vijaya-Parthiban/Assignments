Task 4: CSS Border Styles

Solid Border Example

Solid borders are one of the most common border styles in CSS. They provide a clear, continuous line around the element, making the content stand out. For example, cards on websites, buttons, and input fields often use solid borders to visually separate content from the background. A solid border is simple, professional, and easy to read.

Dashed Border Example

Dashed borders consist of short lines with spaces between them. They are often used for highlighting editable areas, temporary content, or sections that need special attention. For instance, a form that is in draft mode or a section that can be dragged and dropped can use dashed borders to indicate it is interactive. This style adds a light, decorative touch without being too bold.

Double Border Example

Double borders show two parallel lines around an element, creating a decorative and elegant look. They are often used for emphasis, such as highlighting important announcements, quotes, or special offers. Double borders give a sense of sophistication and are less common than solid or dashed borders, making them ideal for sections that need visual distinction. Use them sparingly for the best effect.
